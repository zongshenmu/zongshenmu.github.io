About

Hi! I am a PhD student in Department of Computer Science, Stanford University. I'm advised by Prof. Jure Leskovec. In the past, I have been fortunate enough to work with Prof. Stefano Ermon and Prof. David Lobell as a summer intern. In Tsinghua, I have worked on Machine Learning supervised by Prof. Jun Zhu. My research focuses on developing Machine Learning algorithms for graph/relational structured data, including generating/optimizing graph structures, developing new graph neural network architectures, and applying these techniques in various domains.

Projects

Deep Multi-View Spatial-Temporal Network for Taxi Demand Prediction

Taxi demand prediction is an important building block to en- abling intelligent transportation systems in a smart city. An accurate prediction model can help the city pre-allocate re- sources to meet travel demand and to reduce empty taxis on streets which waste energy and worsen the traffic conges- tion.

Diffusion convolutional recurrent neural network- Data-driven traffic forecasting.pdf

Spatiotemporal forecasting has various applications in neuroscience, climate and transportation domain. Traffic forecasting is one canonical example of such learn- ing task.

GaAN: Gated Attention Networks for Learning on Large and Spatiotemporal Graphs

We propose a new network architecture, Gated Attention Networks (GaAN), for learning on graphs. Unlike the traditional multi-head at- tention mechanism, which equally consumes all attention heads, GaAN uses a convolutional sub-network to control each attention head’s importance.
